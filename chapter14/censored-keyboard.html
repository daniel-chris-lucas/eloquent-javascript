Censored keyboard

Between 1928 and 2013, Turkish law forbade the use of the letters Q, W, and X in official
documents. This was part of a wider initiative to stifle Kurdish culture—those letters
occur in the language used by Kurdish people but not in Istanbul Turkish.

As an exercise in doing ridiculous things with technology, I’m asking you to program a text
field (an <input type="text"> tag) that these letters cannot be typed into.
